The West of Australia

Kokaburra is also called "Lachender Hans" because of its characteristic, hysterical laugh. That is a way to mark their territory in the early morning and late afternoons and believe me, when I say, you rarely get a louder alarm clock.
Humpback whales travel alongside the Australian Coast every winter (June-August) to give birth to their calves in the tropical waters further south. Later that year they return to the Antarctican feeding grounds, that bloom with plancton in summer.
Yardie Creek is one of the most beautiful canyons in Cape Range National Park and habours a lot of animals. Beside of the balck-footed wallaby you can also meet flying foxes, fish eagles and other diverse birds.
