A cry for spring

Fox in the snow - the dream come true for every wildlife photographer

It's so cold, it is nearly impossible to stand still. My fingers are freezing, even though they are packed in my padded, blue gloves and my feet are cold as well, even two pair of socks are not helping against the biting frost. At least it stopped snowing. And while everyone is complaining about the white blanket covering the world and the ice on the streets making it dangerous to drive, I cannot help but admiring the beauty of the silent winter.
The snow of the last weeks was enough to cover the whole grasslands by the small river, only the reed is sticking out and shows the courses of the streams. After melting in the winter sun and freezing during night I try not to fall while constantly slipping on the dense ice, that cracks below the snow. Although I'm looking for foxes, I normally see some deer around this place. They are scratching the frozen earth with their hooves to get at least some green grass. Today as well their tracks are pressed deep into the snow, but not a sign of the small herd that normally roams the region.

Deer is looking for open grassland patches during the forst

Originally I wanted to sit in front of the burrow I knew and wait for the fox to appear, but as soon as my destination comes in sight, I realize, that today I might not need the chair I brought with me at all. Because there against the white snow a red figure is standing out. A fox looking for food!
I'm still nearly half a kilometer away, so I try to get closer without scaring it away. Although it recognizes me while I'm still a world away, it does not seem to mind my presence. Cowering behind a bush I can see it running over the meadow. It looks like it is dancing. For the next minutes I am happily watching it hunting after mice under the snow. Suddenly its attention snaps and I realize way to late, that there is another fox approaching from my direction. Built slightly smaller and more delicate this is probably a female. Her head kept low, the body pressed against the cold snow, she waits for the other to arrive, when suddenly the wind changes and she notices me. Frozen in place, she stares at me, than runs back into the nearby forest patch. The male only looks at her, then turns around and goes back to hunting. Slowly he follows the stream until he is out of my sight.

And me? I'm still totally shocked by this sighting. That was not at all what I expected after I went out the last few days, not even seeing the tip of a tail. A little lightheaded I take a few steps towards the trees when a cry rips through the air. Whirling around, already going down on my knees behind another bush, I can see something shifting on the other side of the reefs, this time on my left side. Red fur flashes through the gras and I cannot believe my eyes as two other foxes appear on the other side of the meadows, playing along the stream, chasing each other around.
