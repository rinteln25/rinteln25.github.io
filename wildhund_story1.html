African wilddogs - Skilled hunters of the bush

A young wilddog at a dam

It´s an early Tuesday morning, the sun just rose. The air already carries the promises of warmer temperatures but right now it is still chill on the back of the pick-up truck. Only the birds are singing while every other organism is just waking up or going to sleep. The whole world is covered in a gold and a feeling of peace is filling the atmosphere. The quiet is only disturbed by the beeping of the telemetry pointing west. They are somewhere on our left side. Even though we are really trying, the bush is to dense to see any trace of them. It´s a surprise we even got a signal at the first try. Our subject? African wilddogs. Fast, kooperative and persistent. You cannot find more successful hunters here.

The scientific name for wilddog is Lycaon pictus

There they are. The whole pack came to the dam, all 10 of them!
